I am Anh Tran, a graduate student at the University of Utah, where I am currently pursuing my PhD in Computer Science with a focus on Medical Image Analysis.<

Research Interests:

  • Computer Vision: I am more specifically interested in medical image analysis, creating 3D models from medical images.
  • Machine Learning: Designing machine learning model to help doctors diagnose patients.

Current Research Project:

  • Tendon's Injury in Professional Athletes:Professional runners could suffer from minor injuries or calcification in their tendons under intense training regime. These could lead to major accidents if not diagnosed early, preventing them from being able to compete in the future or for the rest of their career. Thus, professional athletes will annually check for these injuries. It is a tedious job for doctors to go through hundreds, maybe even thousands of ultrasound to look for these injuries. We want to expedite the process for doctors so they could focus on more important issues.
    As the injuries could be anywhere in the tendon, traditional methods of extracting features would be hard to use, where deep learning would be the right solution. Beside, this is a classic classification problem in which our current goal is to assess whether injury occurs. A neural network architecture based on Convolution layers such ResNets or EfficientNets might be a perfect match, given it is a computer vision problem,.
